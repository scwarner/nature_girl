The following are places in the Midwest where I enjoy visiting for hiking, photography or otherwise communing with nature. This is by no means an exhaustive list. If you have suggestions, please contact me!

Indiana

  • Brown County State Park: Yes, there are hills in Indiana, and this beautiful spot in the southern town of Nashville is a fun spot to hike, especially in the fall.
  • 100 Acres: Nature and art coexist beautifully in this park adjacent to the Indianapolis Museum of Art.
  • Foellinger-Freimann Botanical Conservatory: Located in downtown Fort Wayne, this conservatory features a lush tropical rain forest and an arid desert, all under the glass roof.

Kentucky

  • Bernheim Forest: Located a half-hour south of Louisville, the research forest offers plenty of hiking, along with a scenic lake, an edible garden and interesting sculptures.
  • Parklands of Floyds Fork: This new community park east of Louisville provides ample space to kayak or canoe along a creek, hike along paved and unpaved paths and explore the great outdoors.
  • Mammoth Caves National Park: Here, you can explore some of the world's longest known cave system, but don't forget to enjoy the natural beauty aboveground, as well.

Michigan

  • Fernwood Botanical Garden and Nature Preserve: Located just over the border with Indiana, Fernwood features multiple gardens, including a lily pond and a Japanese garden.
  • Warren Dunes State Park: Lake Michigan and the dunes are the top draws at this spot, located in the southwestern portion of the state.
  • Mackinac Island: Mackinac is famous for its Victorian homes and its fudge, but did you know that 80 percent of the island is a state park? Here, you can explore the island's natural beauty on foot, on bike or on horseback.
